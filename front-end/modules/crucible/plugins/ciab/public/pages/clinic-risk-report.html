          Appendix D · How This Report Was Built

          Methodology

          This assessment combines (1) an intake questionnaire completed by the assessor — covering organizational profile, network topology, endpoint security, account management, and data protection — and (2) a baseline against the CIS Controls v8 Implementation Group 1 (IG1) and NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0. Findings are scored on a 1–5 likelihood × 1–5 impact scale (CIS RAM v2.1 style) producing a 1–25 inherent-risk score normalized to a 0–9 severity band.

          CSF maturity scores are computed by mapping each IG1 safeguard's response (yes / partial / no) to one or more CSF functions via the IG1↔CSF crosswalk, then averaging per function and normalizing to a 0–5 maturity scale. The Compliance Posture archetype is derived from the response pattern and indicates where the organization has invested compared to similar peers. Insurance readiness scoring mirrors the 12-control underwriting questionnaire used by major carriers (Coalition, At-Bay, Cowbell, Resilience).

          Threat source taxonomy (NIST SP 800-30 Rev 1 §3.2): each finding is categorized as Adversarial (human attacker), Accidental (mistake or misconfiguration), Structural (gap in design or process), or Environmental (natural / physical event). This taxonomy aligns with what students will encounter on Security+, CRISC, CISA, and CISSP exams.

          CSF Implementation Tiers (NIST CSF 2.0 §3.2): Tier 1 Partial, Tier 2 Risk Informed, Tier 3 Repeatable, Tier 4 Adaptive. Our 0–5 maturity score maps approximately as: 0–1.5 = Tier 1, 1.5–3 = Tier 2, 3–4 = Tier 3, 4–5 = Tier 4.

          5-point qualitative scale equivalents (NIST SP 800-30 Rev 1 Tables G-2 and H-3): 1 = Very Low, 2 = Low, 3 = Moderate, 4 = High, 5 = Very High. These map directly to the numeric likelihood and impact scales used throughout this report.

          • CIS Controls v8 (IG1) — Center for Internet Security, cisecurity.org/controls/v8
          • CIS Risk Assessment Method (RAM) v2.1 — Mission Impact × Obligations Impact × Likelihood (Expectancy)
          • NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 — six functions: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
          • NIST SP 800-30 Rev 1 — Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments (threat source taxonomy, qualitative scales)
          • NIST SP 800-171 / CMMC — POA&M (Plan of Action and Milestones) format used in POA&M CSV export
          • OCTAVE Allegro (Carnegie Mellon SEI) — asset-centric risk methodology underlying the Asset Register
          • FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk) — Open Group standard, optional quantification basis
          • Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report (DBIR) — empirical basis for SMB threat-scenario library
